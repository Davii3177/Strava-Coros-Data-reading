About Gaman AI

Your training, translated into a clearer next move.

Gaman AI is a personal running workspace for athletes who want more than a list of completed activities. It brings your training history, current workload, race plans, run feedback, shoes, and recovery check-ins together, then turns that context into practical guidance you can inspect and understand.

01 / One training view

See the story behind the miles.

Bring recent Strava activity and Coros-ready imports into one focused view of distance, pace, heart rate, elevation, consistency, records, and race timing.

02 / Daily decisions

Know what today is for.

See one workout, its intended effort, and the evidence behind it. Complete, shorten, modify, or skip the session without losing the context of your week.

03 / Human context

Keep the guidance honest.

Your ratings and recovery check-ins add context a GPS file cannot. Missing metrics stay labeled as unavailable, and model-based suggestions are used only when their safety checks pass.

Live training loop

Every run moves the plan forward.

Activity data establishes the route. Your feedback explains the effort. Gaman AI connects both before recommending what comes next.

Inside the intelligence

Two systems. Two clearly defined jobs.

“AI” does not mean one model controls every decision. Core workout guidance is built from deterministic rules and a small, inspectable model trained only on your rated runs. A separate language model can optionally expand recovery education—but it never controls the urgent-symptom safety gate.

01 / Adaptive workout model

A transparent relationship between the run and how it felt.

Each feedback entry links objective activity data to one subjective target: your difficulty rating. Soreness, motivation, and notes remain useful dashboard context, but the current pace model is trained specifically to estimate perceived difficulty.

How the workout model worksInputs, equation, targets, and fallback

After at least five feedback entries, an inspectable linear regression learns from the runs you rated.

Predicted difficulty intercept + distance coefficient × km + pace coefficient × min/km + elevation coefficient × meters
  1. CollectMinimum five rated runs

    For every saved run rating, the model receives distance, average pace, elevation gain, and your 1–5 difficulty score. Fewer than five entries means no model is fitted.

  2. FitLinear regression

    Scikit-learn fits one intercept and three coefficients. Every learned weight remains inspectable instead of being hidden inside a large neural network.

  3. SolveTarget the intended effort

    The equation estimates pace for a 7 km easy run at difficulty 2/5 and a 5 km tempo run at difficulty 4/5. It adjusts seven-day pace suggestions—not every feature in the app.

  4. ValidateReject unsafe relationships

    A valid fit must learn that a slower min/km pace reduces predicted difficulty. Easy pace must remain slower than tempo pace, and both must be positive.

  5. RefreshUse the current record

    The model is rebuilt from currently saved feedback when the dashboard loads. Adding or deleting a rating changes the next fit.

Before five ratings—or after a rejected fit

Gaman AI uses the visible baseline: recent average pace plus 0.5 min/km for easy running and minus 0.3 min/km for tempo running.

02 / Optional recovery language model

Expanded education after a rule-based safety check.

The Body & Recovery tool can use a configured OpenAI model to turn structured check-in details into cautious, readable guidance. This model is optional; the application still provides conservative built-in guidance if the service is disabled, unavailable, or times out.

How recovery guidance worksContext, safety gate, and service fallback

Context sent when enabled

  • SymptomsBody areas, side, pain level, onset, sensations, movement triggers, location detail, and notes
  • TrainingRecent seven-run distance, prior seven-run distance, percentage change, elevation, and average pace
  • InstructionDiscuss common possibilities without certainty, conservative recovery, gradual return, avoid-for-now items, red flags, and a medical disclaimer
Rule-based safety gateUrgent symptoms bypass ordinary AI guidance.

Pain at 9–10, numbness or tingling, inability to bear weight, deformity, chest pain, breathing trouble, fever, weakness, trauma-related swelling, or bladder/bowel warning terms trigger the built-in urgent-care message instead.

Server-side onlyThe API credentials are never placed in the browser.
Validated inputRegions, symptoms, pain scores, and text lengths are checked.
Rate limitedRecovery requests are limited to five per session hour.
Safe fallbackFailures return conservative built-in guidance.
Current limits, stated plainlyWhat Gaman AI does not know
  • The five-entry threshold allows a fit; it does not prove that the fit is accurate.
  • The workout model currently has no confidence interval, held-out validation score, or injury-risk prediction.
  • Linear regression cannot capture every interaction between terrain, weather, fatigue, sleep, fueling, and pace.
  • The recovery language model can produce incomplete or incorrect text and must not be treated as a diagnosis.
  • Missing splits, cadence, HRV, sleep, and resting heart rate remain unavailable until a source supplies them.

Built for trust

From activity file to next run.

Core analysis uses visible calculations and the feedback-trained workout model. Optional large-model assistance is limited to recovery education when securely configured. Guidance is educational, never a diagnosis, and the runner remains responsible for every training decision.
